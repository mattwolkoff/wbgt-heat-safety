Methodology: WBGT is modeled with the Liljegren et al. (2008) heat-transfer method (the basis of OSHA's WBGT calculator), simulating a black-globe thermometer and wet-bulb wick from temperature, humidity, wind, solar radiation, and pressure — not a statistical formula. Wind is extrapolated from 10 m to the model's ~2 m level using a standard log wind profile, a simplification for terrain where a full stability-class correction isn't available. If the solver doesn't converge for an hour (rare), that hour falls back to a simpler formula, flagged in its detail panel. This is a modeled estimate, not a sensor reading — actual on-field conditions (turf, shade, asphalt) can vary. Flag thresholds are a general-purpose scale for outdoor youth/scholastic sports; defer to your league's official heat policy where one exists. Data from Open-Meteo.
